The Agra Fort is a monumental fortification built primarily of red sandstone, located in Agra, Uttar Pradesh. This UNESCO World Heritage Site served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors until 1638 before the capital shifted to Delhi. It is a perfect blend of military might and architectural splendor.

Commissioned by Emperor Akbar in 1565, the fort was later enhanced by Shah Jahan, who added exquisite marble palaces and gardens. The fort‚Äôs layout is semicircular along the Yamuna River, featuring massive walls that stretch over 2.5 kilometers in circumference.

The Agra Fort comprises several magnificent structures, including the Diwan-i-Am (Hall of Public Audience), Diwan-i-Khas (Hall of Private Audience), and the Moti Masjid (Pearl Mosque). These buildings showcase a unique mix of Persian, Indian, and Islamic architectural styles.

One of the most famous spots within the fort is the Musamman Burj, where Shah Jahan was imprisoned by his son Aurangzeb. From here, he spent his final years gazing at the Taj Mahal, the monument he built for his beloved wife, Mumtaz Mahal.

Today, the Agra Fort stands as a historical jewel, attracting tourists from around the world. It not only narrates tales of Mughal grandeur but also serves as a symbol of India's rich cultural heritage.

Key Highlights:
  • Built by: Emperor Akbar (1565)
  • Architecture: Mughal style with red sandstone and marble
  • Main Structures: Diwan-i-Am, Diwan-i-Khas, Moti Masjid
  • UNESCO Status: World Heritage Site (1983)
  • Location: Along the Yamuna River in Agra
