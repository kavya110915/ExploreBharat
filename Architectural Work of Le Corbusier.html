The Architectural Work of Le Corbusier in Chandigarh represents a modernist masterpiece by one of the most influential architects of the 20th century. As part of the 17 sites across 7 countries inscribed by UNESCO in 2016, the Chandigarh complex stands as a symbol of India's post-independence ambition and architectural innovation.

Designed by Le Corbusier, Chandigarh was India's first planned city. It embodies principles of rational planning, human-centric design, and a unique urban fabric. The Capitol Complex, which includes the Secretariat, Legislative Assembly, and High Court, is a prime example of Brutalist architecture with monumental forms and exposed concrete.
Key Highlights:
  • UNESCO Status: World Heritage Site (2016)
  • Architect: Le Corbusier
  • Main Structures: Secretariat, High Court, Legislative Assembly
  • Architecture Style: Modernist and Brutalist
  • Urban Significance: First planned city in post-independence India
  • Global Recognition: Part of 17 Corbusier sites across 7 countries
Visitor Information:

Best Time to Visit: October to March

Access: Public areas are accessible; some buildings require special permission

Nearest Airport: Chandigarh International Airport

Tips: Visit the Open Hand Monument and take a guided architecture tour
