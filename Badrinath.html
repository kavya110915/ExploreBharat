Badrinath Temple

Badrinath Temple is one of the most revered Hindu shrines dedicated to Lord Vishnu. It is part of the Char Dham pilgrimage and is located in the scenic town of Badrinath.
Fun Fact:
~The temple is believed to be over 1,000 years old and is situated at an altitude of 3,133 meters! Things to Explore at Badrinath Temple:

  • Spiritual Experience:
    Participate in the morning and evening aarti (prayer ceremonies) for a divine experience.
  • Tapt Kund:
    Take a dip in the natural hot springs near the temple, believed to have healing properties.
  • Mana Village:
    Visit the last inhabited village before the Indo-China border, located just 3 km from Badrinath.
  • Neelkanth Peak:
    Admire the stunning views of Neelkanth Peak, which is often covered in snow.

Why Visit Badrinath Temple?
!Because it’s not just a pilgrimage site but also a place of immense natural beauty and spiritual significance.

Mana Village

Mana Village is the last inhabited village before the Indo-China border, located just 3 km from Badrinath. It’s known for its scenic beauty and cultural significance.
Fun Fact:
~Mana is often referred to as the 'Last Indian Village' and is a gateway to several trekking routes! Things to Explore at Mana Village:

  • Vyas Gufa:
    Visit the cave where Sage Vyas is believed to have composed the Mahabharata.
  • Bhimpul:
    See the natural rock bridge formed by Bhima, one of the Pandava brothers.
  • Local Culture:
    Experience the unique culture and traditions of the local people.
  • Scenic Views:
    Enjoy breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

Why Visit Mana Village?
!Because it’s a perfect blend of culture, history, and natural beauty, making it a must-visit destination near Badrinath.

Badrinath Festival

Badrinath Festival is a grand celebration held annually in Badrinath, marking the opening of the Badrinath Temple after the winter season. It’s a vibrant festival filled with cultural performances, rituals, and spiritual significance.
Fun Fact:
