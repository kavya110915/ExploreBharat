Raigad Fort

Raigad was the capital of the Maratha Empire under Shivaji Maharaj. It was here that he was coronated as Chhatrapati. The fort is located atop a hill and offers stunning views, with a ropeway now providing access to the top.

Key Features:
  • Capital fort
  • Coronation site
  • Ropeway access

Rajgad Fort

Rajgad served as Shivaji‚Äôs capital for over 25 years before shifting to Raigad. Its massive structure and strategic location made it nearly impregnable. The fort has several watchtowers, palaces, and water tanks.

Key Features:
  • Strategic height
  • Multiple citadels
  • Longest capital

Pratapgad Fort

Pratapgad is historically known for the battle between Shivaji Maharaj and Afzal Khan. The fort has a majestic statue of Shivaji and scenic views of the surrounding forested hills.

Key Features:
  • Battle site of Afzal Khan
  • Strategic location
  • Panoramic views

Sindhudurg Fort

Built on an islet in the Arabian Sea, Sindhudurg is a coastal fort known for its robust construction and concealed main entrance. It houses a rare shrine of Shivaji Maharaj himself.

Key Features:
  • Coastal sea fort
  • Hidden entrance
  • Shivaji temple

Suvarnadurg Fort

Located near Dapoli in Ratnagiri, Suvarnadurg was built to protect the coastline from foreign invasions. It was an important naval base under Kanhoji Angre.

Key Features:
  • Naval base
  • Strategic sea location
  • Linked to mainland

Lohgad Fort

Lohgad is known for its iron-like strength and the unique Vinchukata (scorpion-tail shaped) fortification. It was captured by Shivaji Maharaj in 1648 and served as a treasury fort.

Key Features:
  • Scorpion-tail shape
  • Monsoon trekking spot
  • Massive gates

Salher Fort

Salher is the highest fort in Maharashtra and was the site of a major battle between the Marathas and Mughals. It played a key role in Shivaji‚Äôs northern expansion.

Key Features:
  • Highest fort in Maharashtra
  • Battle of Salher
  • Panoramic views

Shivneri Fort

Shivneri is the birthplace of Shivaji Maharaj. The fort is well-planned with water reservoirs, prayer rooms, and a temple dedicated to goddess Shivai.

Key Features:
  • Birthplace of Shivaji Maharaj
  • Hilltop planning
  • Fortified walls

Khanderi Fort

Khanderi is an island fort in the Arabian Sea, built to monitor enemy movements along the coast. It has a functioning lighthouse and was part of Shivaji‚Äôs naval strategy.

Panhala Fort

Panhala is one of the largest forts in the Deccan and was captured by Shivaji from the Adil Shahi dynasty. It has a rich history of battles and Maratha administration.

Key Features:

  • Historic siege
