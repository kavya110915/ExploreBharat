Jantar Mantar in Jaipur is an astronomical observatory built in the early 18th century by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II. It is one of five observatories he constructed across India, but the Jaipur Jantar Mantar is the largest and best preserved. It consists of 19 architectural astronomical instruments, including the world's largest stone sundial.

Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2010, Jantar Mantar is not only a scientific marvel but also a demonstration of the intellectual and cultural advancements of India during the early modern period. The instruments are fixed and used to measure time, track celestial bodies, and predict eclipses with remarkable accuracy.

Each structure within the complex is a tool with a specific purpose in astronomical observation. Unlike modern telescopic observatories, Jantar Mantar uses massive masonry instruments to gather and study celestial data. This blend of science, architecture, and art makes it an enduring symbol of India's rich scientific heritage.
