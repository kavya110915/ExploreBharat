The Khajuraho Group of Monuments is one of the most remarkable architectural wonders of India, located in the state of Madhya Pradesh. These temples are renowned worldwide for their stunning sculptures, intricate carvings, and artistic brilliance, depicting a blend of spirituality, symbolism, and sensuality.

Built between 950 and 1050 CE by the Chandela dynasty, the Khajuraho temples are divided into three groups: Western, Eastern, and Southern. Out of the original 85 temples, around 20 still survive, standing as a testimony to India's cultural and architectural heritage.

The temples are famous for their erotic sculptures that represent a celebration of love and life, alongside depictions of everyday life, mythical beings, and deities. The Kandariya Mahadeva Temple is the largest and most ornate of them all.
