Khangchendzonga National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in the Indian state of Sikkim. Nestled amidst the majestic Himalayas, the park is named after the third highest mountain in the world ‚Äî Mount Khangchendzonga (8,586 m). It is renowned for its stunning alpine landscapes, rich biodiversity, and spiritual significance in local traditions.

The park is home to a wide range of flora and fauna, including red pandas, snow leopards, Himalayan black bears, and over 500 species of birds. From glaciers to lush forests, the park offers breathtaking views and an immersive experience in nature.

Khangchendzonga is also culturally important for the Sikkimese people, with several sacred sites and traditional beliefs deeply connected to the region. It is one of the few mixed (natural and cultural) heritage sites in the world.

Basically Khangchendzonga National Park is home to diverse wildlife, including snow leopards, red pandas, and various bird species. The park's unique geography and climate support a wide range of flora and fauna. Visitors can enjoy trekking, camping, and exploring the park's stunning landscapes.
Key Features:
  • Area: 849.5 sq km
  • Altitude Range: 1,829m to 8,586m
  • Fauna: Red panda, snow leopard, Himalayan tahr, musk deer
  • Flora: Rhododendrons, alpine meadows, temperate forests
  • UNESCO Status: Mixed Heritage Site (Natural & Cultural) since 2016
Visitor Information:

Best Time to Visit: March to May and September to November

Permits: Required for Indian and foreign tourists (can be arranged in Gangtok)

Activities: Trekking, wildlife spotting, nature walks, and cultural exploration

How to Reach: The nearest airport is Bagdogra (West Bengal), followed by a drive to Yuksom or Gangtok
