Nalanda Mahavihara (Nalanda University)

üìç Nalanda, Bihar, India
Nalanda Mahavihara, one of the oldest and greatest centers of learning in ancient India, flourished from the 5th to 12th century CE. It was a renowned Buddhist monastic university that attracted scholars and students from Tibet, China, Korea, and Central Asia.

This historic institution included temples, lecture halls, dormitories, meditation centers, and libraries. It represents the zenith of ancient Indian educational and spiritual traditions and has been recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site for its architectural layout and intellectual legacy.
Key Highlights:
  • UNESCO Status: World Heritage Site (2016)
  • Founded: 5th Century CE during Gupta Empire
  • Academic Influence: Attracted students from across Asia
  • Infrastructure: Temples, viharas, classrooms, and libraries
  • Legacy: Preserved in travelogues of Xuanzang and I-Tsing
  • Rediscovered: 19th century by British archaeologists
