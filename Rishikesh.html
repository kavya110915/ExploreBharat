Triveni Ghat

Triveni Ghat is a sacred bathing ghat on the banks of the Ganges River in Rishikesh. It is famous for its evening Ganga Aarti, where devotees gather to witness the spiritual ceremony.
Fun Fact:
~The ghat is named after the confluence of three sacred rivers: Ganga, Yamuna, and Saraswati! Things to Explore at Triveni Ghat:

  • Evening Aarti:
    Experience the mesmerizing evening Aarti ceremony with chanting and lamp offerings.
  • Spiritual Atmosphere:
    Feel the spiritual energy and tranquility of the ghat.
  • Local Markets:
    Explore nearby markets for local handicrafts and souvenirs.
  • Photography:
    Capture stunning photographs of the ghat and the river.

Why Visit Triveni Ghat?
!Because it’s a perfect blend of spirituality, culture, and natural beauty in Rishikesh.

Laxman Jhula

Laxman Jhula is a famous suspension bridge in Rishikesh, connecting the two banks of the Ganges River. It is named after Lord Rama's brother, Lakshmana, who is believed to have crossed the river here.
Fun Fact:
~The bridge is an architectural marvel and offers stunning views of the river and surrounding hills! Things to Explore at Laxman Jhula:

  • Scenic Views:
    Enjoy breathtaking views of the Ganges and the surrounding landscape.
  • Local Temples:
    Visit nearby temples like the Laxman Temple and Tera Manzil Temple.
  • Shopping:
    Explore local shops for souvenirs, handicrafts, and spiritual items.
  • Photography:
    Capture stunning photographs of the bridge and the river.

Why Visit Laxman Jhula?
!Because it’s a perfect blend of spirituality, culture, and natural beauty in Rishikesh.

Ram Jhula

Parmarth Niketan Ashram

Parmarth Niketan is one of the largest and most renowned ashrams in Rishikesh. It offers yoga, meditation, and spiritual teachings, along with a serene environment by the Ganges.
Fun Fact:

Neelkanth Mahadev Temple

Beatles Ashram

The Beatles Ashram, also known as Chaurasi Kutia, is a former ashram where the Beatles stayed in the late 1960s. It is now a popular tourist attraction known for its graffiti art and serene surroundings.
Fun Fact:
~The ashram was a place of meditation and creativity for the Beatles during their visit to India! Things to Explore at Beatles Ashram:

  • Graffiti Art:
    Explore the vibrant graffiti art created by various artists on the walls of the ashram.
  • Meditation Spots:
    Find peaceful spots for meditation and reflection within the ashram premises.
  • Historical Significance:
    Learn about the history and significance of the ashram during the Beatles' visit.
  • Photography:
    Capture stunning photographs of the unique architecture and art within the ashram.

Why Visit Beatles Ashram?
!Because it’s a unique blend of music history, art, and spirituality in Rishikesh.
