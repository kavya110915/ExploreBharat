The Sacred Ensembles of the Hoysalas represent some of the most intricate and detailed temple architecture in India. Located in Karnataka, these temples were built between the 12th and 13th centuries during the reign of the Hoysala dynasty and stand as masterpieces of South Indian art and craftsmanship.

Temples like the Chennakeshava Temple in Belur, the Hoysaleswara Temple in Halebidu, and the Keshava Temple in Somnathapura are prime examples of the distinctive Hoysala style, which is characterized by star-shaped platforms, highly detailed stone carvings, and iconography from Hindu epics.

The Hoysalas employed soapstone for their construction, which allowed for exquisite and intricate sculptural work. The temples are adorned with depictions of gods, goddesses, dancers, musicians, and animals, making them living showcases of India‚Äôs cultural and religious traditions.

These sacred ensembles were not just places of worship but also centers of learning and art. The temples reflect a fusion of Dravidian and Nagara architectural influences, which make them unique among the temple traditions of India.

In 2023, UNESCO recognized the Sacred Ensembles of the Hoysalas as a World Heritage Site for their architectural excellence and historical significance, ensuring their protection and global recognition.
