The Sundarbans National Park is one of the largest mangrove forests in the world and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Located in West Bengal, it spans the delta formed by the Ganga, Brahmaputra, and Meghna rivers, creating a network of tidal waterways, mudflats, and small islands covered with dense mangroves.

This unique ecosystem is globally renowned for being home to the iconic Royal Bengal Tiger. The park is also inhabited by saltwater crocodiles, fishing cats, wild boars, and an incredible variety of bird species, making it a paradise for wildlife enthusiasts and re ers alike.

The name "Sundarbans" is derived from the Sundari trees (Heritiera fomes), which are the most abundant species of mangrove found in the area. These trees, along with the rich biodiversity, play a vital role in protecting the coastal regions from cyclones and tidal waves.

Besides its ecological importance, the Sundarbans is a cultural hub, with fishing communities and boatmen who have adapted to the challenging tidal environment. Due to its fragile ecosystem, conservation efforts are ongoing to protect the Sundarbans from the threats of climate change, rising sea levels, and human encroachment. Eco-tourism is encouraged here to create awareness while preserving its natural beauty and unique wildlife.

Key Highlights:
  • Famous For: Home of the Royal Bengal Tiger
  • UNESCO Status: World Heritage Site
  • Biodiversity: Mangroves, crocodiles, migratory birds, and aquatic wildlife
  • Rivers: Ganga, Brahmaputra, and Meghna delta region
  • Activities: Boat safaris, wildlife photography, and bird watching
Visitor Information:

Best Time to Visit: November to March for comfortable weather

Entry Fee: Varies based on safari packages and permits

Activities: Eco-tourism, guided tours, and river cruises

Tip: Boat rides during early morning hours increase the chances of spotting tigers and wildlife.
