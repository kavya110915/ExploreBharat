Brihadeeswarar Temple

üìç Thanjavur, Tamil Nadu
The Brihadeeswarar Temple, also known as the Big Temple, is a grand architectural masterpiece built during the reign of the Chola dynasty in the 11th century. Dedicated to Lord Shiva, this UNESCO World Heritage Site stands as one of the most significant landmarks of South Indian temple architecture.

Commissioned by Raja Raja Chola I in 1010 CE, the temple is renowned for its towering Vimana (temple tower) which stands at approximately 66 meters, making it one of the tallest in the world. The temple is constructed entirely of granite, with intricate carvings and sculptures depicting various deities and mythological stories.

The temple complex also houses massive monolithic statues, including a colossal Nandi (bull) carved out of a single stone. Brihadeeswarar Temple is not only a place of worship but also a monument reflecting the artistic, cultural, and engineering prowess of the Chola Empire.
Key Highlights:
  • Deity: Lord Shiva
  • Architecture: Dravidian style with towering Vimana
  • Built in: 1010 CE by Raja Raja Chola I
  • Heritage Status: UNESCO World Heritage Site
  • Special Feature: Entirely built from granite with no nearby quarries
