The Chhatrapati Shivaji Terminus (formerly Victoria Terminus) is a majestic blend of Victorian Gothic Revival and traditional Indian architecture. Located in the heart of Mumbai, this UNESCO World Heritage Site is not just a railway station but a historic symbol of India's colonial past and architectural brilliance.

Designed by British architect Frederick William Stevens and completed in 1887, CST was constructed to commemorate the Golden Jubilee of Queen Victoria. Its stunning facade, intricately carved with Indian motifs, turrets, pointed arches, and stained glass windows, resembles a European cathedral.

CST is one of the busiest railway stations in India, connecting millions of people daily, yet it manages to preserve its heritage charm. The station's grand central dome, sculptures, and ornamental ironwork are engineering marvels.
