The Elephanta Caves, located on Elephanta Island near Mumbai, Maharashtra, are a network of sculpted caves dedicated primarily to the Hindu god Shiva. These caves are a UNESCO World Heritage Site and are known for their impressive rock-cut architecture and intricate carvings that date back to the 5th to 8th centuries.

The most iconic sculpture is the Trimurti Sadashiva, a 20-foot depiction of a three-headed Shiva symbolizing the creator, preserver, and destroyer aspects of the god. The main cave also includes other elaborate depictions of Shiva in various forms.

The island can be accessed via ferry from the Gateway of India and offers a tranquil escape into ancient Indian art, religion, and history.
