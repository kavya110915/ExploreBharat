Great Himalayan National Park

The Great Himalayan National Park (GHNP), located in the beautiful Kullu region of Himachal Pradesh, is a natural treasure nestled in the western Himalayas. Established in 1984 and declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2014, this park covers an area of over 1,100 square kilometers, offering a unique blend of rich biodiversity and stunning alpine landscapes.

GHNP is home to over 375 species of fauna, including rare animals like the snow leopard, Himalayan brown bear, musk deer, and western tragopan. The terrain is marked by rugged mountains, thick coniferous forests, river valleys, and serene meadows. It's a dream destination for trekkers, wildlife enthusiasts, and nature lovers.

The park's isolation from human interference makes it an excellent conservation model. Visitors can embark on multi-day treks through untouched wilderness, interact with local Himalayan communities, and witness the changing ecological zones from pine forests to glaciers.

Its raw natural beauty, ecological importance, and thrilling trekking routes make GHNP a hidden gem in India's natural heritage. A visit here is not just a travel experience but a journey into the pristine heart of the Himalayas.
Key Features:
  • Established: 1984
  • UNESCO Site: Since 2014
  • Wildlife: Snow Leopard, Musk Deer, Himalayan Tahr, Blue Sheep
  • Activities: Trekking, Wildlife Photography, Eco-tourism
  • Ecology: Alpine meadows, Riverine forests, Glaciers
Visitor Information:

Entry Permit: Required from the Forest Department

Best Time to Visit: April to June and September to November

How to Reach: Nearest airport: Bhuntar (Kullu); Nearest railhead: Joginder Nagar; Road access from Kullu and Aut.

Trekking Base: Sai Ropa Forest Complex
