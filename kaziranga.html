Kaziranga National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in the Indian state of Assam. Renowned for its successful conservation of the Great Indian One-Horned Rhinoceros, Kaziranga spans across the floodplains of the Brahmaputra River and is home to a stunning diversity of wildlife.

The park's landscape features tall elephant grass, marshland, and dense tropical forests, making it a haven for not just rhinos, but also for tigers, elephants, wild buffaloes, and swamp deer. It's also recognized as an Important Bird Area (IBA) by BirdLife International due to its rich avian population including storks, pelicans, and eagles.
