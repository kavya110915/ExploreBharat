Keoladeo National Park, also known as Bharatpur Bird Sanctuary, is a spectacular avian paradise located in the state of Rajasthan. Originally a royal duck hunting reserve, it was declared a national park in 1982 and later recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

The park is home to over 375 species of birds, including rare and migratory species such as the Siberian Crane. Its wetland ecosystem supports a diverse range of flora and fauna, making it one of the most important bird breeding and feeding grounds in the world.

Visitors can explore the serene environment on foot, bicycle, or by rickshaw. The lush vegetation, peaceful water bodies, and symphony of birdcalls make it an enchanting destination for bird watchers and nature lovers alike.

The sanctuary plays a crucial role in wetland conservation and serves as a model for sustainable ecological practices in India. The magical sight of thousands of birds fluttering across the skies is a soul-stirring experience for every nature enthusiast.
Key Features:
  • Type: Bird Sanctuary & Wetland Reserve
  • UNESCO Status: World Heritage Site since 1985
  • Popular Species: Siberian Crane, Painted Stork, Pelicans, Herons
  • Activities: Bird watching, nature walks, cycle and rickshaw safaris
  • Best Time to Visit: October to March for migratory birds
