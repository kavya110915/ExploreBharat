Manas Wildlife Sanctuary

üìç Assam, India
The Manas Wildlife Sanctuary is a breathtaking UNESCO World Heritage Site nestled at the foothills of the Himalayas in Assam. Known for its lush landscapes, tropical moist forests, and rich biodiversity, Manas is both a Project Tiger Reserve and a Biosphere Reserve, making it a haven for wildlife lovers and conservationists.

The sanctuary is home to a remarkable variety of wildlife, including the endangered Assam Roofed Turtle, Golden Langur, and the elusive Bengal Tiger. With the Manas River flowing through the park, the sanctuary boasts stunning natural beauty with grasslands, hills, and woodlands that support a complex ecosystem.

Manas offers visitors a thrilling experience with jeep safaris, elephant rides, and birdwatching opportunities. Its unique location near the Indo-Bhutan border also gives it a rich blend of flora and fauna rarely found elsewhere in the world.
Key Features:
  • Designation: UNESCO World Heritage Site
  • Famous For: Tigers, Elephants, Rhinos, Rare Birds
  • Habitat: Grasslands, Sub-Himalayan forests, Wetlands
  • Activities: Jeep Safari, Bird Watching, Eco-Tours
  • Nearby Attractions: Bhutan Hills, Bansbari Tea Garden
