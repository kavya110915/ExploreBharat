The Nanda Devi and Valley of Flowers National Parks together form a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in the Chamoli district of Uttarakhand. These parks represent the stunning natural beauty of the Western Himalayas and preserve unique high-altitude ecosystems.

The region is home to a rich diversity of flora and fauna, many of which are rare or endangered. From alpine meadows and glacial basins to snow leopards and blue sheep, these parks are both ecologically and spiritually significant.
Nanda Devi National Park

Nestled around India‚Äôs second-highest mountain, Nanda Devi (7,816 m), this park is characterized by rugged terrain, steep gorges, and breathtaking alpine scenery. Declared a national park in 1982, it is a restricted-access zone that has helped preserve its fragile ecosystem.

The park serves as a sanctuary for Himalayan wildlife such as snow leopards, Himalayan tahr, musk deer, and bharal. It is also a site of cultural reverence for many local communities.

  • UNESCO Status: World Heritage Site (1988)
  • Area: 630.33 sq. km
  • Key Highlights: Nanda Devi Peak, glacial valleys, rare fauna
Valley of Flowers National Park

Famous for its vibrant alpine meadows, the Valley of Flowers blooms with thousands of endemic flower species during monsoon months. It is located at an altitude of over 3,500 meters and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 2005 (extension of Nanda Devi site).

The valley remains covered in snow for most of the year, but from June to September, it transforms into a paradise of color and fragrance. Trekking to this region offers a surreal experience surrounded by Himalayan beauty.

  • UNESCO Status: World Heritage Site (2005)
  • Famous For: Alpine flowers, meadows, scenic trekking
  • Key Species: Brahma Kamal, Blue Poppy, Himalayan Monal
