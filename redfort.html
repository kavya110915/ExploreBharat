The Red Fort, or Lal Qila, is a majestic historical fortification located in the heart of Old Delhi. Built in 1648 by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan, it served as the main residence of the Mughal dynasty for around 200 years.

Crafted from stunning red sandstone, this iconic structure is a symbol of India‚Äôs rich past and architectural brilliance. With towering walls, beautifully ornamented gates, and detailed marble work, it reflects the grandeur of Mughal design. The fort's planning combines Islamic, Persian, Timurid, and Indian architectural elements, creating a harmonious masterpiece.

Every year, on India's Independence Day, the Prime Minister hoists the national flag here, making it a powerful national symbol. Inside the fort complex, visitors can explore beautiful gardens, the Diwan-i-Aam (Hall of Public Audience), Diwan-i-Khas (Hall of Private Audience), museums, and former royal chambers.

Today, the Red Fort stands not only as a historical site but also as a UNESCO World Heritage Site that captures the spirit of India‚Äôs cultural and political evolution. Walking through its vast corridors and intricate halls, one can feel the echoes of history and the grandeur of India‚Äôs past.
