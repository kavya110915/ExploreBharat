Western Ghats

üìç Maharashtra, Goa, Karnataka, Kerala, Tamil Nadu

The Western Ghats, also known as the Sahyadri Hills, form one of the most ancient mountain ranges in the world, predating even the Himalayas. Spanning over 1,600 kilometers across the western coast of India, they stretch through Maharashtra, Goa, Karnataka, Kerala, and Tamil Nadu. This UNESCO World Heritage Site is renowned for its extraordinary biodiversity and cultural significance.

Often called the "Great Escarpment of India," the Western Ghats are home to numerous rivers that originate from these mountains, including the Godavari, Krishna, and Kaveri. These rivers not only sustain agriculture but also provide drinking water and hydroelectric power to millions of people in southern and western India.

The region is considered a global biodiversity hotspot, with thousands of species of flora and fauna, many of which are endemic to the area. Its lush forests, sprawling grasslands, and rare ecosystems support wildlife such as tigers, elephants, lion-tailed macaques, and Malabar civets.

Besides natural beauty, the Western Ghats hold immense cultural and historical significance. Ancient temples, forts, and pilgrimage sites are scattered across its range. Hill stations like Munnar, Wayanad, and Mahabaleshwar attract tourists seeking cooler climates and stunning landscapes.

Due to its ecological sensitivity, the Western Ghats face challenges like deforestation, mining, and urbanization. Conservation efforts, eco-tourism initiatives, and UNESCO recognition aim to preserve this unique natural heritage for future generations.

Key Highlights:
  • Length: 1,600 km across five states
  • Biodiversity: Over 7,400 species of plants and animals
  • UNESCO Status: Recognized as a World Heritage Site
  • Rivers: Godavari, Krishna, Kaveri, Periyar
  • Tourist Spots: Munnar, Coorg, Wayanad, Mahabaleshwar
Visitor Information:

Best Time to Visit: October to March for trekking and sightseeing

Entry Fee: Depends on the specific national parks or sites visited

Activities: Trekking, wildlife safaris, tea plantation tours, and cultural exploration

Tip: Monsoon months (June to September) offer lush greenery but heavy rainfall.
